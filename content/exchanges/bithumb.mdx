## Bithumb: Korea’s High-Liquidity KRW Gateway for Digital Assets

Founded in 2014 and headquartered in Seoul, Bithumb is one of South Korea’s longest-running cryptocurrency exchanges. Operated by BTC Korea.com Co., Ltd., it has grown into a leading fiat on-ramp for KRW spot markets, pairing deep local liquidity with a retail-friendly interface focused on simplicity and speed. With a primary emphasis on spot trading of major cryptocurrencies and Korean-won pairs, Bithumb competes directly with domestic peers while offering a distinct blend of low-latency execution, bank-integrated deposits, and strong compliance with South Korean financial regulations.

As a mainstream entry point for crypto trading in Korea, Bithumb is designed to make buying and selling **[Bitcoin](/posts/what-is-bitcoin)**, **[Ethereum](/posts/what-is-ethereum)**, and a broad list of **[altcoins](/posts/what-are-altcoins)** feel familiar to users accustomed to local fintech and banking experiences.

## Core Services

Bithumb centers its product set on efficient spot markets, reliable KRW rails, and a curated lineup of coins favored by Korean retail and institutions. While the global exchange landscape includes multi-product giants with derivatives and DeFi-style yield, Bithumb’s differentiation lies in its compliance-led, KRW-focused approach.

- **Spot Trading:** Core order book markets across KRW and crypto-quoted pairs, with 200+ digital assets supported and hundreds of tradable markets. Market, limit, and conditional orders are available, with an emphasis on high-throughput matching for active traders.

- **KRW On/Off-Ramps:** Integration with real-name Korean bank accounts enables fast KRW deposits and withdrawals for eligible residents. Fiat rails are designed to comply with domestic travel rule standards and bank-level KYC/AML procedures.

- **Altcoin Coverage:** Beyond majors like **[Bitcoin](/posts/what-is-bitcoin)** and **[Ethereum](/posts/what-is-ethereum)**, Bithumb lists a broad set of **[altcoins](/posts/what-are-altcoins)** that see active trading interest in the Korean market. Listings typically prioritize liquidity, regulatory stance, and community demand.

- **Simple Earn and Events:** While Bithumb does not position itself as a yield-first platform, it periodically offers promotional earn events and coin-specific programs. For networks using **[Proof-of-Stake](/posts/what-is-proof-of-stake)**, Bithumb may enable staking-like features when feasible, though terms, durations, and eligible assets vary. Users seeking a foundational overview can revisit the basics of **[staking](/posts/crypto-staking)** and understand how yields differ across assets.

- **API and Market Data:** REST and WebSocket APIs for programmatic access, including order placement, account data, and candlesticks. This supports quant strategies and third-party tooling with latency-sensitive execution.

- **Mobile and Web UI:** Native iOS and Android apps mirror the streamlined web interface, including watchlists, market depth, charts, and one-tap order placement optimized for KR retail behavior.

- **Institutional and OTC Support:** Bithumb has historically supported higher-touch services for larger trades and corporate clients, focusing on execution quality and settlement predictability under local compliance frameworks.

- **What Bithumb Is Not:** Bithumb is not primarily a derivatives venue. Leverage and futures access are limited or unavailable, reflecting South Korea’s regulatory posture toward retail derivatives. Users who need perpetuals or advanced leverage often compare domestic spot-first experiences with global multi-product platforms, or consider alternative venues that fit their jurisdictional constraints. For domestic spot liquidity, many traders also evaluate **[Upbit](/exchanges/upbit)** as a nearby point of comparison in Korea’s KRW market.

## Security Measures

Security on Bithumb spans account controls, infrastructure protections, and regulatory-mandated data safeguards shaped by Korea’s oversight of virtual asset service providers.

- **Account Security:** Two-factor authentication (TOTP/SMS), device management, withdrawal address whitelisting, and anti-phishing codes are available. Users can harden access further by employing unique passphrases and segregating emails used for exchange logins from personal accounts.

- **Custody and Cold Storage:** Bithumb custody policies emphasize multi-signature controls and majority cold storage for client funds. Hot wallet percentages are kept to operational minimums, with regular key rotation and strict withdrawal checks.

- **Compliance Certifications:** South Korean exchanges must satisfy rigorous information security standards (e.g., ISMS) and operational audits. Bithumb has aligned its controls with these frameworks, including encryption at rest/in transit and access hygiene for privileged systems.

- **Incident History and Response:** Bithumb has experienced notable security events. In 2017, a data breach exposed user information; in June 2018, approximately tens of millions of dollars’ worth of assets were stolen from hot wallets. The exchange paused services, conducted investigations, enhanced controls, and publicly addressed reimbursement and recovery procedures. While these incidents are part of Bithumb’s historical record, they also catalyzed broader security investments. Users should continuously evaluate their own risk posture, including when to keep long-term holdings in self-custody.

- **User Safety Practices:** Regardless of exchange safeguards, crypto users benefit from best practices. For long-term storage, consider hardware wallets and multisig options; here’s a primer on **[how to store crypto](/posts/how-to-store-crypto)** securely. When navigating listings and promotions, stay vigilant about red flags and avoid **[crypto scams](/posts/how-to-avoid-crypto-scams)** that target unwary traders during volatile markets.

## Regulatory Compliance

Bithumb operates under South Korea’s regulatory regime for virtual asset service providers (VASPs). The exchange has registered with the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) under the Act on Reporting and Using Specified Financial Transaction Information, and it adheres to the “real-name” account system for fiat on/off-ramps.

- **Licensing and Oversight:** VASP registration, ISMS certification, and ongoing audits are central to Bithumb’s compliance stack. The exchange implements the domestic travel rule with whitelisting of counterparties in supported jurisdictions.

- **Bank Partnerships:** Real-name KRW accounts must be opened with a partnered bank to enable fiat transfers. This banking linkage is a hallmark of Korean exchanges and ties fiat flows to strong KYC/AML controls.

- **Geographic Availability:** Bithumb’s KRW services target residents with eligible Korean bank accounts. Offerings may be restricted in certain jurisdictions, and derivatives products are limited or unavailable in line with local policy.

- **Comparative Context:** For US-based users who need a heavily regulated on-ramp, exchanges such as **[Coinbase](/exchanges/coinbase)** or other US-licensed venues may be more appropriate. Within Korea’s spot-first environment, traders often weigh Bithumb’s order books against domestic competitor **[Upbit](/exchanges/upbit)** to evaluate fees, coin coverage, and promotions.
