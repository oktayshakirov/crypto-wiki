## LBank: A Global Altcoin-Focused Cryptocurrency Exchange

Founded in 2015, LBank is a global cryptocurrency exchange best known for fast listings of emerging tokens, broad market coverage, and a feature set that caters to both retail and professional traders. Headquartered via offshore registrations with operational hubs across Asia and the Middle East, LBank targets users who want early access to markets while still offering mainstream trading in assets like **[Bitcoin](/posts/what-is-bitcoin)** and **[Ethereum](/posts/what-is-ethereum)**. In positioning, LBank sits closer to altcoin-centric platforms—think a middle ground between **[KuCoin](/exchanges/kucoin)** and **[MEXC](/exchanges/mexc)**—with a strong emphasis on listings, perpetual futures, and accessible mobile trading.

## Core Services

LBank’s core value proposition is depth and speed of market access alongside a full trading stack. The exchange combines a large-asset spot market, derivatives with competitive leverage, yield/earn features, and listing programs for new tokens.

- **Trading Platform:** Advanced and lite interfaces on web, iOS, and Android; customizable layouts, watchlists, and multi-chart view. Offers REST/WebSocket APIs for market data and execution, plus sub-accounts for structured team access.
- **Spot Trading:** Hundreds of cryptocurrencies and well over a thousand trading pairs, especially in small- and mid-cap **[altcoins](/posts/what-are-altcoins)**. Core majors and stablecoins (BTC, ETH, USDT, USDC) act as base pairs for most markets.
- **Perpetual Futures:** USDT- and coin-margined contracts on top majors and popular narratives (AI, RWA, L2). Leverage typically up to 100x depending on pair and risk tier; cross and isolated margin modes; built-in risk engine with auto-deleverage logic when liquidity thins. Note: leverage can amplify gains and losses—position sizing and risk controls matter.
- **Margin and Borrowing:** Cross/isolated margin on select spot pairs; borrowable assets rotate based on liquidity. Interest rates float with market conditions; liquidation levels are transparent in the order ticket.
- **Fees and VIP Tiers:** Maker/taker spot fees commonly start around 0.10%/0.10%, with reductions via higher 30-day volume or VIP tiers. Futures fees typically follow a maker/taker structure around 0.02%/0.06% (pair-dependent). Users can apply fee discounts through loyalty programs or the platform token where available.
- **Earn and Staking:** Flexible savings, fixed-term deposits, and staking for **[Proof-of-Stake](/posts/what-is-proof-of-stake)** assets. The “Earn” page usually shows APYs, lock periods, and redemption rules; yields vary and may involve third-party protocols.
- **Launchpad/Launchpool:** Access to primary market sales and liquidity campaigns for early-stage projects, generally gated by KYC, regional access rules, and sometimes balance snapshots or ticketing.
- **Copy Trading:** Strategy following for futures traders with leaderboards, PnL statistics, and risk parameters; copy allocations can be capped per position and follower account.
- **Fiat Access and P2P:** Third-party on-ramps and P2P desks are available in selected regions. Availability and fees vary by provider and jurisdiction.
- **Research and Tools:** Market dashboards, listings calendar, and category pages for narratives; for additional context on market breadth and momentum, many traders complement exchange data with tools like the **[Crypto Heatmap](/tools/crypto-heatmap)** and sentiment via the **[Fear and Greed Index](/tools/fear-and-greed-index)**.
- **Staking Support:** Simplified staking flows for major PoS networks are integrated in-app; users new to yield concepts may want to review fundamentals in **[staking](/posts/crypto-staking)** and **[DeFi](/posts/what-is-defi)** before committing funds.

## Security Measures

Security at LBank covers account protections, platform-level defenses, and operational processes. While the exchange emphasizes multi-layer security, users should remember that any centralized venue is a custodial counterparty.

- **Account Security:**
  - **2FA and MFA:** Google Authenticator/SMS/email 2FA, mandatory for withdrawals and critical actions.
  - **Withdrawal Whitelists:** Optional address whitelisting restricts outbound transfers to approved destinations.
  - **Anti-Phishing Codes:** Custom mail code to authenticate official emails and reduce spoof risk.
- **Custody and Wallet Architecture:**
  - **Cold/Hot Wallet Segregation:** The majority of funds are typically held in cold storage with controlled access; hot wallets support operational liquidity.
  - **Operational Controls:** Multi-signature approvals for large movements; internal monitoring for anomaly detection.
- **Proof-of-Reserves and Transparency:** LBank has published reserves snapshots and wallet attestations at intervals. As with many CEXs, these are not the same as a continuous, auditor-verified, real-time solvency audit. Treat PoR as one data point among many.
- **Incident Response and Continuity:** Exchange-level security programs include 24/7 monitoring, incident playbooks, and third-party security partnerships. As with all platforms, downtime can happen during peak volatility; set alerts and plan for volatility-driven spreads.
- **User Best Practices:** For long-term storage, consider self-custody—see guidance on **[how to store crypto](/posts/how-to-store-crypto)**—and stay vigilant against social engineering via **[crypto scams](/posts/how-to-avoid-crypto-scams)**. Use strong, unique passwords and keep recovery codes offline.

## Regulatory Compliance

LBank serves a global audience under an offshore registration structure and partners with third-party payment providers in select regions. As a result:

- **Licensing Footprint:** LBank does not position itself as a fully licensed exchange in tightly regulated markets like the US. It implements KYC/AML controls, sanctions screening, and transaction monitoring consistent with global standards for offshore exchanges.
- **Regional Availability:** Product availability (especially derivatives, Launchpad access, and P2P) varies by jurisdiction. The platform may restrict service to residents of certain countries; it is your responsibility to check local laws and LBank’s Terms of Use.
- **Tax and Reporting:** LBank offers statements and export tools; however, users should consult local regulations and tax professionals to determine reportable events.
- **Comparative Note:** Traders who prioritize strong, domestic licensing—especially in the US—often compare LBank’s offering with regulated options like **[Coinbase](/exchanges/coinbase)** and similar national exchanges. The trade-off is generally fewer small-cap listings but a tighter compliance envelope.
